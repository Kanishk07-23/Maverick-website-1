SEO has a reputation problem.

Most people think it's slow, expensive, and impossible to attribute to revenue. That's what incumbents want you to believe — because it keeps you dependent on paid channels where they take a percentage of your spend.

Here's the reality: **targeted SEO can generate qualified leads within 90 days** for most B2B and D2C startups. It just requires different thinking than what most agencies sell you.

## The Myth of "Domain Authority Takes Time"

Domain Authority (DA) is a Moz metric. Google doesn't use it.

What Google does use is a complex combination of relevance signals, backlink quality, and topical authority. And topical authority is something you *can* build fast — if you focus on the right cluster.

Here's our 90-day sprint framework:

## Phase 1: Nail Your Topical Cluster (Days 1–14)

Don't try to rank for everything. Pick one narrow topic cluster where you can credibly be the best resource on the internet.

**Example:** A brand selling ayurvedic supplements doesn't try to rank for "supplements." They own "ashwagandha for stress relief" — a cluster of 8–12 tightly related queries with clear commercial intent.

**Deliverables by Day 14:**
- 1 pillar page (2,500+ words, covers the cluster comprehensively)
- 6–8 supporting articles (800–1,200 words each, targeting specific long-tail queries)
- Internal linking structure that funnels authority to the pillar

## Phase 2: Technical Foundation (Days 14–30)

Brilliant content in a broken site goes nowhere. Our technical audit checklist:

- **Core Web Vitals** — LCP under 2.5s, CLS under 0.1, INP under 200ms
- **Crawl budget** — no orphan pages, no duplicate content, proper canonicals
- **Schema markup** — at minimum, Organization, LocalBusiness, and Article schema on relevant pages
- **Mobile-first indexing** — if it doesn't work perfectly on a ₹8,000 Android, you have a problem

## Phase 3: Intent-Matched Content (Days 30–90)

This is where most agencies fail their clients — they write content for Google bots, not for humans with problems.

The three intent types that drive conversions:

| Intent | Example Query | Content Type |
|---|---|---|
| Informational | "how to reduce cortisol naturally" | Guide / Explainer |
| Comparative | "ashwagandha vs rhodiola for stress" | Comparison Article |
| Commercial | "best ashwagandha supplement India" | Product Round-up |

Most startup content calendars are 80% informational. The highest-converting sites balance all three.

## The Link Building Reality Check

You don't need 1,000 backlinks. You need 15–20 contextually relevant links from sites your audience actually reads.

Our approach:
- **Digital PR** — original data/research that journalists want to cite
- **Guest contributions** — not spammy "guest posts" but genuine expert pieces in relevant publications
- **Resource page outreach** — find curated resource pages in your niche and pitch inclusion

One high-quality link from Economic Times or Your Story moves the needle more than 50 directory submissions.

## Measuring What Matters

Forget rankings as your primary KPI. Measure:

1. **Organic traffic to money pages** (product, pricing, contact)
2. **Organic-to-lead conversion rate**
3. **Keyword impressions for target cluster** (via Google Search Console)
4. **Revenue attributed to organic** (UTM + CRM integration)

Rankings are a leading indicator. Revenue is the outcome.
